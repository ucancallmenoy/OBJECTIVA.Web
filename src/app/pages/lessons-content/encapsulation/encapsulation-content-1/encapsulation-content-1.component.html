What is Encapsulation?

Encapsulation means putting data and the functions that work on that data together in one unit, called a class. It also controls who can access the data and functions by using clear rules.

Encapsulation acts like a shield that blocks outside access to the class's data and functions, keeping them safe and secure.

Data Hiding and Security

Encapsulation is closely linked to data hiding, where internal details of an object are concealed from the outside. This helps:

  • Protect sensitive information.
  • Prevent accidental interference with the object's state.
  • Provide a clear and structured way to interact with data through methods.

    • Imagine an ATM: You interact with its buttons and screen (methods) without knowing the inner workings of its hardware (data hiding).

    What are the benefits of encapsulation?

    The main benefit of encapsulation is that it lets you re-factor or improve the code inside a class without messing up other parts of the program. This makes the code easier to update and maintain.

    Security

    Restricts unauthorized access to the object's state.

    Flexibility

    You can change the internal logic of methods without affecting external code.

    Maintainability

    By keeping data and behavior in one place, encapsulation simplifies debugging and updates.

    Reusability

    Encapsulated classes can be reused without worrying about external interference.

    Real-world examples of encapsulation

    Here are some of the real-world examples of the concept of encapsulation:

    Bank Account: Your balance is private, but you can deposit or withdraw money using specific methods.

    Mobile Phone: Internal components like the processor are hidden. You interact through a touchscreen or buttons.
